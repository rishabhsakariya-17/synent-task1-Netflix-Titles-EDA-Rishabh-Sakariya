📌 Project Overview

This project is submitted as part of the Synent Technologies Data Science Internship Program.

The objective of this project is to perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Netflix Titles Dataset to identify trends, patterns, and insights related to Netflix content.

Using Python and data visualization techniques, the project analyzes content distribution, ratings, countries, genres, directors, actors, and growth trends over time.

📊 Exploratory Data Analysis

The following analyses were performed:

1. Movies vs TV Shows Analysis

Compared the distribution of Movies and TV Shows available on Netflix.

2. Content Rating Distribution

Analyzed audience ratings such as TV-MA, TV-14, PG-13, and R.

3. Top Producing Countries

Identified countries contributing the most Netflix content.

4. Content Growth Trend

Analyzed content additions over different years.

5. Genre Analysis

Identified the most popular genres available on Netflix.

6. Top Directors Analysis

Found directors with the highest number of Netflix titles.

7. Top Actors Analysis

Analyzed actors appearing most frequently on Netflix.

8. Correlation Analysis

Generated correlation matrix and heatmap for numerical features.

📈 Key Findings
Netflix contains significantly more Movies than TV Shows.
TV-MA and TV-14 are the most common ratings.
United States contributes the highest amount of content.
India is one of the leading content-producing countries.
Content additions increased rapidly after 2015.
Dramas, Comedies, and International Movies dominate Netflix.
💡 Business Insights
Netflix strongly focuses on movie content.
Mature audience content forms a large portion of the catalog.
International content plays a major role in platform growth.
Content acquisition increased significantly between 2016–2020.
Regional content from countries like India contributes heavily to engagement.
